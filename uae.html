Paradise: The Isle of Skye

The Isle of Skye is a rugged and beautiful island located in the northwest of Scotland. It is the largest of the Inner Hebrides islands and is known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Geographically, Skye is characterized by its mountainous terrain, with the Cuillin Range dominating the island's skyline. The island also has numerous lochs, including the famous Loch Coruisk, which is surrounded by steep cliffs and waterfalls. The island's culture is deeply rooted in Gaelic tradition, and the language is still spoken by some of the residents. The island has a rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back thousands of years. Skye has also played an important role in Scottish history, with many battles and conflicts taking place on the island. In terms of attractions, Skye has many natural wonders, such as the Fairy Pools, the Old Man of Storr, and the Quiraing. The island is also home to many historic sites, such as Dunvegan Castle and the Museum of Island Life.

One the way to The Isle of Skye

9 a.m., We left Edinburgh to begin our highland journey, and we are scheduled to arrive in the evening on the Isle of Skye. I could image this will be a long with the weather getting worse and worse. I was expecting three days of rain on our highland expedition. Unsurprisingly, the sky has changed from blue to grey since we left Edinburgh. The rain became heavier as we travelled. My mood is easy to be influenced by the weather, and thus I thought I won’t be excited on my highland trip, but I still tried to make myself enjoy every moment during the trip. The rain didn’t stop for around six hours, and thus I guessed the weather of the Isle of Skye would be worse. What’s more, I didn’t sleep well last night, so I thought I would fall asleep while arriving the hotel. Surprisingly, since we arrived Fort William, the largest city before going to the Isle of Skye. The blue sky started to say hello and welcome us. Interestingly, I became excited again and looked forward to the days on the Isle of Skye. After 90 minutes ride, we finally arrived the Isle of Skye, and the weather was sunny as expected. We also got an opportunity to watch sunset when we arrived Portree, the largest city on the Isle of Skye. How lucky we were!!!

Nature and Wildness

The next day, we spent all way on the Isle of Skye. It was another sunny day. I really cherished this moment because it is rare in Scotland, not to mention the Isle of Skye. The first spot we went was Fairy Pools. It entices visitors from all over the world every year, as they make some great ‘Wild Swimming’ for those brave enough to enter the cold water. For me, I was tolerated enough of cold weather in Canada, and thus I chose to walk on the trails and took fantastic photos only. The thing that amazed me the most in Fairy Pools was glacial terrain. For a person who grew up in a tropical island. It was really rare to see glacier in person. The knowledge I had for glaciers was from my geography class, but I could see the U-shaped valley in person while walking on the trails. This was so amazing and I told myself I should be humbler in front of mother nature. The power of mother nature is really impressive.

Gaelic tradition and Scotland cultures

The Gaelic language, also known as Scottish Gaelic, is still spoken by a small number of people on the island. Signs throughout Skye are often bilingual, with both English and Gaelic names for places and landmarks. Gaelic is also taught in some schools on the island, and there are efforts to preserve and promote the language. This can be seen on the way to Skye from Edinburgh. When we entered the highland, the first line of road sign is Gaelic instead of English. This made me remind my trip in Cape Breton in Canada, another island with many Gaelic population and spending many efforts to preserve and promote Gaelic. After watching sunset in Portree, we encountered a parade on the main street. It was traditional performance of Scotland marching band. When I was in Edinburgh, I have been to the Edinburgh Military Tattoo. It was great and unbelievable. However, I prefer to watch the one while walking on the street because it is more localized and traditional.

Islandness of the Isle of Skye

The trip to Skye was unforgettable, I got three sunny days when I was there, it was really rare in Scotland. I saw the glacier landscape while I was there, and experienced the strong wind from Nordic Ocean in person. The soil there was poor, and thus the economy of Skye is highly depending on tourism. Most of people went to Fort William to work in the off-season, and this made this island more depressed and wilder. I would say the islandess of Skye is cloudy weather (although I didn’t meet it, I still can image) nature, glacier, seafood, whiskies, wind and Gaelic as well as Scotland cultures.
