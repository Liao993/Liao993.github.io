Why did I choose to come to Prince Edward Island?

The 15 years and half of my flying career passed so fast, and in the last 2 years of them I felt like it was time to do something else. Flying is very addictive, and even though it has been almost 3 years since I left it, I still miss it badly. I started to realize that I need to start doing something completely different, because I would not be flying forever. I thought maybe I should make a career shift, or go back to university and study something interesting. I rested my mind on going back to studies, however I wanted like to do that in a different way. I wanted to do my studies out of my home country, and I wanted to do it in a place that is so different and unfamiliar to me. Then, I started doing my research about that, and I came across the program of island studies at Prince Edward Island University. I said that was it, I have to go there. I was interested in the nature of the program itself as it was concerned with studying islands, and I was also so impressed with the idea of going and spending two years in Prince Edward Island.

I was supposed to be doing my studies starting September 2020, however due to the Covid-19 pandemic, I asked the university if I could come in September 2021 instead, and luckily it was fine and they accepted that. In that year before coming here, I started reading a lot about Prince Edward Island, and thought it was kind of a very island beautiful destination with lots of scenery. At that point, I had no clue about islands in an academic way the way I’m now, I was not even aware that islands are categorized as warm water and cold water islands. I was not that knowledgeable about the effect of climate change on the environment, and on the ecosystem. I had no idea also about over-tourism and the danger of that on islands, and also the scarcity of natural resources and the importance of protecting that. When I was flying to any islands, I was like any other tourists or visitors who were just there to enjoy the destination and have fun, and do not care about anything else more or less.

When I started my island studies program, I started seeing islands in a different way. I saw the bigger picture about all this. Doing these studies really enlightened me a lot, and if it happens that I could visit any islands again in the future, I would think and behave in a different way. As they always say: Knowledge is Power, and this is so true.

Facts I discovered about Prince Edward Island when I arrived there

I arrived on the 26th of August 2021. My start here was not a very pleasant one, as they put me in quarantine for 14 days, and I got released on the 7th of September, which was only one day before the start of classes. It would have been a great idea to discover the island in those 14 days, which was also the end of the summer season, and to have an idea about the new place that I would live in. Unfortunately, I was not allowed to leave the quarantine at all, so basically I felt as if I was in a kind of confinement. My hotel room did not even have a nice view, and I was facing the parking lot as I remember.

Another thing I was not aware of, is that you need to drive to all the tourist attractions, and they are very far away from the city of Charlottetown where I was living. You have to have a car or rent one, or go with a friend. I started looking for tourism companies that arrange tours and provide pick up and drop off, but they were all closed because of the covid pandemic. I was so frustrated, and all I was doing was discovering Charlottetown only through walking. When we started classes, I became friends with two of my classmates Helena and Henry (Henry is the one I’m doing this website project with; he is my project partner). Helena had a car and we managed to go out a couple of times only, because we were so busy with our studies, and the weather started to get cold. With Henry as well, we used to explore things in Charlottetown together through cycling, and sometimes when he takes the car from his cousin. Henry is an international student like me, and he is from Taiwan, and it was also his first time in Prince Edward Island. Helena was from Ontario and originally from the Azores Islands, and it was her first time in Prince Edward Island as well. I was happy that I made two friends who are originally from island backgrounds.

Things I have done so far in Prince Edward Island

I still have to explore Prince Edward Island properly, and check out many places I have not been to yet. I live in Charlottetown, which is considered the capital of Prince Edward Island. I'm lucky that I live in the heart of the city, and I explored most of its surroundings. I have been to the Confederation Centre of Arts, which is located downtown and offers some art and heritage programs that talk about the history of the place and about canada. I have also visited the Victoria Park Boardwalk, which was luckily close to all the apartments I rented, and I used to go there to exercise or simply walk on the edge to meditate and reflect about things. The Fall season is so beautiful in Prince Edward Island, and the tree leaves start to have the colors of yellow, brown, maroon, gold and copper before the fall off, and seeing that was so magical. I have been with my friend Henry to Bonshaw Hills Provincial Park, as we walked some trails there to check the fall colorful leaves. We also did some trail cycling as well, we went to the Sherwood Trail, and we rode bicycles there, as the weather was still sunny and nice.

With Helena, I went on a long road trip with her, and Helena loved checking out the Lighthouses and wanted to see the one at West Point. Prince Edward Island has a lot of Lighthouses. We drove all the way there, which was a very long drive, and we got lost so many times, however, we were checking other stuff on our way as well. We stopped by Cow’s Factory, and it was often referred to as Canada’s best ice cream. We saw how they made other things there such as chocolates, t-shirts and cheese, we had some delicious ice cream of course as well from there. On our way to West Point, we stopped to take pictures with the windmills in Miscouche, as there were plenty of them. Because we got lost so many times on our way to West Point Lighthouse, we managed to arrive there by sunset, and we captured very nice photos and videos. On our way back, we were so hungry and we said we would eat in Charlottetown, and it was quite late so only the Hunter’s Ale House was open. The food was not that great there, but Helena and I were so busy talking about our trip and checking out the photos we took.

There are other few places that I also visited with organized tours by university, because being in the island tourism program, we got so lucky to go out and see some interesting things. Together with my classmates we went to visit the Beaconsfield Historic House, and there you will get to see how life was during the Victorian Era, and what the Victorian houses looked like inside, and their lifestyle and habits. We also went on another trip to Port Charlottetown, to get an idea about cruise tourism. At that time we went there, it was closed due to the covid pandemic, but was planned to open in the summer of 2022. We also checked the surroundings there, and we entered Receiver's Coffee at Founders Mall to have some morning pastries and coffee. We were all chilled in the outdoor seating area while having them. We had an assignment where each one of us had to go to a place of our own choice, and then write a paper about what we experienced there. I had chosen Port-La-Joye Fort Amherst, as it was a place of an important significant history of Prince Edward Island, and I highly recommend it, as it gives a better understanding of important historical incidents that happened there.

Other times I went out was during winter, as I had my sister and brother in law visiting me in December 2022. They wanted to see the famous attractions of Prince Edward Island such as: Cavendish, Anne of Green Gables House, Greenwich Beach, Sandspit Amusement park, Shining Waters Family Fun Park and the Haunted Mansion. I kept on telling them that these places are seasonal and they are only open during the summer time, however they still insisted on going there. They said they wanna check these places out even if they are closed, to get an idea about them and plan another trip in summer. I also did not manage to visit any of them myself during the summer, because as I explained above you need a car to go there. The thing I liked the most when my sister visited me was that I got to see the Confederation Bridge from a distance, and we also visited the Air Force Heritage Park in Summerside, where they have a collection of military aircraft displays that were used many years ago.

I managed to find a part time job working as a night auditor, in a great historic boutique hotel that is very well known in Charlottetown, and it is called: The Great George Hotel. I became friends with so many wonderful colleagues there, some of them are from the island, while others were international students like in my case. My best friend from there is Oliver, and we managed to go out a lot, especially to the different restaurants of Charlottetown. We have been to The Merchant Man, Piatto, Cedar’s Eatery, Papa Joe’s, Sim’s Steakhouse, The Gahan House, Ta Ke Sushi and Water’s Edge. I recommend visiting the Great George Hotel, as it is a very iconic place, and they have the famous St. Dunstan’s Basilica Cathedral in front of it, so both could be visited at the same time.

Islandness in Prince Edward Island

Since Prince Edward Island is my first experience on a cold water island, Islandness and its meanings are kind of different when it comes to it. Unlike tropical islands that are always sunny, and usually when the place is sunny it brings with it feelings of happiness and warmth, Prince Edward Island gives me a bit of bitterness. I Love Prince Edward Island so much though, since I have been here for almost two years now. However, maybe because it is a cold water island and seasonal, I keep on thinking that I'm away from everyone that I know. There are oceans and miles between here and my home country, where my family are. I think islanders or people who have their families with them will have different feelings towards it, maybe they have a certain bond towards it, and they do not have those gloomy or somber thoughts about the island.

Also, I managed to experience feelings of vulnerability and resilience during the huge Fiona Hurricane that happened last September 2022. It was a great learning experience for me to witness how islands are so vulnerable, and that people should be together, and have a sense of the power of community, in order to overcome the obstacles they are prone to face on islands.

Prince Edward Island is my first cold water island experience, I really would like to explore more cold water ones. I'm hoping that my next cold water island destination will be Iceland, as it was always my dream to visit it. I would like to go there for like 10 days to explore the wonderful tourist attractions, as I tend to go crazy whenever I see or read anything related to Iceland. My love for islands is forever, and I'm so grateful that I had the opportunity to visit some of them; whether they were tropical, cold or artificial ones. They are all great and worth being seen and explored.
