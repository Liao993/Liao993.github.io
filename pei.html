Ocean

PEI is renowned for its stunning coastline, which includes countless beaches and the vast expanse of the Atlantic Ocean. From the deep blues of the ocean to the soft white sands of the beaches, there is much to love about the natural beauty of Prince Edward Island's ocean and beach landscapes. One of the most popular beaches on the island is Brackley Beach, which features a long boardwalk that runs along the sand and provides stunning views of the ocean. The beach is also surrounded by lush green forests and rolling hills, adding to the natural beauty of the area. Prince Edward Island's beaches are relatively quiet and uncrowded, providing the perfect setting for a peaceful day by the water. Visitors can relax in the sun, read a book, or simply watch the waves roll in and out, enjoying the peaceful serenity of the ocean and beach environment.

Brackley, Stanhope Beach and Charlottetown Harbour with my friends

Last June, also my first summer in PEI. I got a chance to join beach cleanup activity. While walking on the Stanhope beach in PEI National Park. The water was warm and transparent, but what made me more surprised was the sand. It was white, clean, and sparkling. Generally, the beach is really clean, just some waste from fisheries activities. I am glad that I could have this chance to cooperate with Park Canada to prepare clean beaches for tourists in the following traveling season.

Laying on a beach to watch sunset and star was another unforgettable thing I did in PEI. When my friend, Jason visited in June. I took him to Brackley beach to watch sunset. After the sun went down to the ocean. The sky started to be dark, and this is the moment I am waiting for. There is no light on PEI beach, so it provides a perfect place for star watching. Tens of thousands of stars were on the sky at that night. It is difficult to take the pics of that star sky via Phone Camera, it is one of the things must to do in person. If there is any other friend visited me in the future in summer, I will take them to watch stars again.

Red Sand and Potatoes

The red sand in PEI is also one of the famous island characteristics. The soil gets its characteristic red color from the high iron oxide content. PEI red sand has several properties that make it ideal for agriculture. PEI red sand is known for its high nutrient content, and this is why potatoes are so famous in PEI. Red sand is porous and allows for good drainage of water, which can help prevent issues such as waterlogging and rotting of potato crops. It is also known to have a lower risk of soil-borne diseases compared to other types of soil. This can help reduce the risk of diseases affecting potato crops, which can lead to higher yields and better-quality potatoes. the Confederation Bridge and the connection to mainland

I still remembered my first time to visit the Confederation Bridge, which is the longest bridge in the world crossing ice-covered water. This bridge crosses of the Northumberland Strait, linking the province of Prince Edward Island (PEI) with the province of New Brunswick on the mainland. The weather on that day was still cold, around zero degree. The wind was so really strong that I thought I could be blown away. However, I still chose to get off the car, just for taking memorable pictures. This was my first time to see ice floating on the water, and thus I must take some photos to grasp that special moment no matter how cold it was.

Confederation Bridge winter and summer time and Northumberland Strait

Before going to the Bridge, I had no idea how importance of the bridge to PEI. I remembered there was a session about the Bridge in my first class, Themes and Perspectives in Island Studies. At that time, I could not understand why islanders were so excited that the bridge opened to traffic on May 31, 1997. The Bridge is just a transportation infrastructure, but why islander can be so emotional in that discussion? Since I visited the bridge, I could understand that feeling. Without the bridge, I could image that islanders are really isolated in the winter time. I had the same feeling for my first winter in PEI. I could feel loneliness and isolated while living in Prince Edward Island, where is no so much fun to do here in winter. I felt bored and kind of stuck on the island until I took a road trip to Halifax, and this was the chance for me to visit the Bridge in winter. After crossing the Bridge, I started to feel that I reconnected to the world. Because of the winter, the covered ice on the ocean, the freezing temperature and few friends here at that time, I felt more isolated while living in Prince Edward Island. I even asked myself why I do not live in Taiwan instead. The winter in Taiwan is warm and I have more fun things to do there. Therefore, I could understand why the bridge is so important after going through my first winter in PEI.

In the June, I got my second chance to visit the Bridge, and I went there with my Taiwanese friend, who flew from Illinois to Prince Edward Island just for visiting this beautiful island. I was like a local people and took a tourist to visit several tourist spots on the island, and the bridge is one of the top-rated spots here. I still remembered that the weather was so lovely at that day. While the wind from the Northumberland Strait kissed on my cheek from time to time. How enjoyable the live is! My friend and I sat on the bench and watched cars entering PEI one by one. It looked like retired people who enjoy their summer beside the coast. The sunshine splatters on the sea and make the blue brighter. The view of the bridge on that day was so breathtaking. I am still struggling to find a word to describe it. The feeling on that day made me understand that the season great for going to the coast is back. In other words, the touristâ€™s season of PEI is back, and the Bridge would take its responsibility to bring road-trip tourists to PEI. PEI became more energetic again and it was not a lonely island, which made me feel isolated in the winter.

The bridge reminds me the physical broader of an island. In the daily life, I do not go to the coast so often, and thus it is difficult to tell I am living in mainland or island. While getting close to the coast, I started to remember I am living in an island and ocean is still a physical obstacle for me to leave island by car. Fortunately, the construction of bridge can make island reconnect to mainland again, but I never have this experience before coming to PEI. Taiwan is an island as well, but the size of it is much larger than that of PEI. Therefore, I could not feel that I am actually on an island, not to mention the feeling of loneliness what I had here in the winter time. However, when I want to leave Taiwan, I need to go to the airport. Therefore, my connection of leaving an island is always an airport. By contrast, people in PEI gets a chance to leave here by car. People can drive 13 kilometers and arrive New Brunswick or even the whole Canada. This experience is new to me. The Bridge made me realize that I can go to the coast to leave an island instead of going to an airport.

After the one-day trip, Paul has shared some differences he found between U.K. and PEI. I only listed three examples here. The first one is the design of houses. Some Victorian style houses can be found in PEI, but he still could tell the difference from those in U.K. Secondly, tips culture is uncommon in U.K., and thus he was not used to it when we dined in. Islandness of PEI

After living in PEI one and half year. I wanted to share how I feel about PEI. Generally, my Isandness of PEI is quietness, peace, seafood, ocean, red sand, splendid beaches and British cultures. The influence of Britain can still be found in Charlottetown. It is similar that Taiwan has kept many Chinese cultures. Quietness and peace is why I feel so comfortable while living in PEI, but if the winter went too long, It made me feel lonely instead, especially in the end of March and the early April. The spring is supposed to come, but why we still get snow during that period. At that moment, I did have a strong motivation to escape to tropical islands.
